Six Modules — COMPLY SB 1288

Every requirement in Education Code 33328.5

Each module presents CSBA model policy language, assesses your district's current status, captures your customizations, and collects the compliance certifications required by the statute. Complete all six to unlock the Review & Generate stage.

A

Academic Integrity

Policies governing plagiarism, AI-generated content disclosure, and academic honesty in the context of AI tools across grade levels.

Anchors 33328.5(d)(4)(A)

B

Acceptable Uses

Clear guidelines for appropriate and inappropriate AI use by students and educators. Covers grade-level differentiation and equity in access.

Anchors 33328.5(d)(4)(B) & (H)

Data Privacy

FERPA, CCPA, and SOPIPA compliance framework. Data use agreements, privacy assessments, vendor certification, and breach notification protocols.

D

Parent Access

Notification requirements, opt-out processes, and parent and guardian rights to access information pupils enter into AI systems.

Anchors 33328.5(d)(4)(D)

Procurement

Vendor vetting standards, contract provisions, AI tool registry, and annual review cycles. Produces the district's AI Tool Registry as a standalone artifact.

Anchors 33328.5(d)(4)(E)

F

Human Oversight & Professional Development

Required human review before AI influences student decisions. Professional development standards and effectiveness evaluation for educators.

Anchors 33328.5(d)(4)(F), (G) & (I)

GAMUT Hosts the Policy

CSBA's GAMUT platform carries 650+ board policies for California LEAs. COMPLY generates AI policies that are formatted to integrate directly into GAMUT's policy record structure — BP number, section headings, legal references, mandated/conditionally-mandated flags.

COMPLY Generates & Verifies It

COMPLY produces the board-ready policy document through the six-module workflow and verifies compliance against every 33328.5(d)(4) requirement, A through I. The audit trail documents each certification with timestamp, module reference, and supporting attachment.

Annual Refresh Cycle

A district's policy is not a one-time artifact. When CSBA publishes model policy updates, CDE issues new guidance, or statutory amendments occur, COMPLY flags every affected district and initiates a pre-populated refresh run covering only the changed material.

The Subscription Mechanic

The first COMPLY run is the heavy lift. Every annual refresh is incremental — twenty minutes, not a full session. This is the operational difference between selling a policy generator and selling a compliance platform. COMPLY is the latter.

What COMPLY Produces

Three deliverables, board-ready at generation

Every completed COMPLY SB 1288 run generates a full artifact set. The board-ready policy document, the AI tool registry, and the GAMUT ingestion payload are produced simultaneously at the Review & Generate stage.

COMPLY SB 1288 — Artifact Set

  • Board-ready AI governance policy document (.docx)
  • CSBA board policy format: BP heading, section text, legal references, revision history
  • District customizations integrated with CSBA model language
  • FERPA / CCPA / SOPIPA compliance certifications with timestamps
  • Parent notification template in plain English
  • AI Tool Registry (.xlsx) — pre-populated from Module E responses
  • Procurement documentation for COE and auditor review
  • Human approval workflow specifications
  • Statutory audit trail: section-by-section against 33328.5(d)(4)(A)–(I)
  • GAMUT ingestion payload (JSON) for direct policy library integration

All artifacts carry the ComplyRun ID and generation timestamp. The board-adopted final version, once captured, supersedes the generated draft as the policy of record.

The Help Branch

A real person is one click away, at every screen

COMPLY is not a self-service tool districts have to figure out alone. A persistent Help affordance appears on every screen of the workflow. One click opens an immediate-response channel.

Tier 1 — AI assistance, instant. Trained on COMPLY documentation, SB 1288 statutory text, and CSBA model policy language. Handles the majority of questions in under two seconds, around the clock.

Tier 2 — H-EDU staff, immediate. When a question requires legal interpretation, involves a complex local circumstance, or the user asks for a person, the conversation escalates to a live H-EDU staff queue. The staff member sees the full conversation history, the user's current module location, and the district's prior COMPLY work. They can answer — or complete the user's work alongside them.

For California districts with small staff and no AI policy precedent, this is the difference between a tool that gets adopted and one that doesn't.
